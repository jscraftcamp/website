Our Community Values

Creating a welcoming, respectful, and inclusive space for everyone

Our Commitment to You

The JSCraftCamp community is committed to providing a friendly, safe, and welcoming environment for all participants. We believe that everyone deserves to feel comfortable, respected, and valued during our event.

We invite all those who participate in JSCraftCamp to help us create positive experiences for everyone. These values guide our community interactions and help us maintain a space where people can learn, share, and grow together.

Communication

We engage in thoughtful discussions, listening actively and considering different viewpoints.

We do not tolerate discrimination of any kind, including but not limited to discrimination based on gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, age, religion, or technical experience level.

Community Guidelines

Encouraged Behaviors

  • Treat all attendees as equals. We're all humans learning together. Assume the person next to you is just as capable as you are, regardless of their appearance or background.
  • Be welcoming, friendly, and patient. Treat everyone you meet with respect and kindness. Ask questions before jumping to conclusions about someone's knowledge or experience.
  • Be professional and constructive. Disagreements are opportunities for learning, not for putting others down. If a discussion becomes heated, take a step back and focus on "I" statements about your perspective.
  • Be mindful of your words and their impact. Remember that strong criticism of tools and technologies can make others feel unwelcome if they use those tools. Frame opinions as personal preferences rather than universal truths.
  • Use inclusive language. Choose words that welcome everyone. Consider using terms like "folks," "everyone," or "friends" instead of gendered terms like "guys." If someone suggests a more inclusive term, take it as helpful feedback.

Behaviors to Avoid

  • Harassment or intimidation of any participant in any form.
  • Discriminatory jokes or language related to gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, mental health, physical appearance, body size, race, or religion.
  • Unwelcome physical attention or contact without explicit consent.
  • Disruptive behavior during sessions or deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following.
  • Insults or put-downs of other participants or their contributions.

Getting Help

If You Experience or Witness a Problem

If you experience or witness behavior that violates our community values:

  • Speak up if you safely can, politely asking the person to stop the concerning behavior.
  • If you don't feel comfortable addressing it directly, or if the behavior continues, please contact a member of the organizing team immediately.
  • All reports will be handled with discretion and taken seriously.

How We'll Respond

The organizing team will:

  • Listen carefully to your concern and collect necessary information
  • Speak with all involved parties to understand the situation
  • Take appropriate action based on the severity of the violation
  • Follow up with the reporting person about the resolution

Potential responses may include a conversation about behavior, a formal warning, or if necessary, asking someone to leave the event. In severe cases, we may contact relevant authorities.
